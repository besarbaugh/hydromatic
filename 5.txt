Execution, Time Management & Communication Discipline

CDRR Pillar 6: Talent & Engineering Excellence

Objective: Build disciplined habits across time management, delivery execution, and communication — ensuring work is estimated accurately, delivered completely, and communicated concisely.

A. Adopt a Time Management System
Read Smart Brevity (VandeHei) and complete Time Management Fundamentals on LinkedIn Learning (Dave Crenshaw) by end of Q2. Adopt a daily time-blocking practice; protect 3+ hrs/day for deep work on priority workstreams from Q3 onward. Publish 1 Jive post (Q3) sharing the adopted system and how it applies to Cloud IAM work.

B. Delivery Discipline & Estimation Transparency
Maintain prioritized Jira backlog with estimates on all stories/epics. Cap concurrent workstreams at 3 (WIP limit). Achieve 80%+ Say/Do completion rate on committed sprint deliverables by Q3 — no carryover without proactive re-scoping communicated 2+ weeks early. Weekly Friday review (~15 min): time audit, Jira status, priority alignment. Review completion trends in biweekly 1:1s. Share selective stop-doing list at mid-year and year-end.

C. Communication & Meeting Effectiveness
Before each meeting as presenter/key contributor, prepare 3-bullet outline: headline, ask/decision, one layer of detail on request. Lead with conclusions; let audience pull for depth. Pause 2-3 seconds before responding to reduce interrupting. Calibrate depth to audience. Apply Smart Brevity as communication framework. Solicit lightweight peer feedback (2-question Slack check-in) from 3 peers at mid-year and year-end.

Success Criteria:
- Time-blocking system adopted and in daily use by Q2
- WIP limit of 3 concurrent workstreams maintained
- 80%+ Say/Do sprint completion rate by Q3
- Headline-first framework applied in all stakeholder meetings
- Positive peer feedback on communication conciseness at mid-year
- 1 Jive blog post published (Q3)
- Manager confirmation of improvement at mid-year

SAY/DO RATIO EXPLAINED
Track the percentage of Jira stories/points committed at sprint start that are completed by sprint end without carryover. Target: 80%+. This measures reliable execution without the overhead of logging exact hours against estimates. Reviewed in biweekly 1:1s with Michael to show trend over time.

PEER FEEDBACK APPROACH
Identify 3 peers (mix of CIAM squad + cross-team). Two lightweight rounds:
- Mid-Year (Aug/Sep): 2-question Slack message — "Have you noticed improvement in my communication conciseness and meeting prep? Any blind spots?"
- Year-End (Nov/Dec): Same format. Compare to mid-year. Include in self-assessment.
No formal survey. Takes peers <2 minutes. Document responses.
