Execution, Time Management & Communication Discipline

CDRR Pillar 6: Talent & Engineering Excellence

Objective: Build disciplined habits across time management, delivery execution, and communication — ensuring work is estimated accurately, delivered completely, and communicated concisely.

A. Adopt a Time Management System
Complete learning path: The One Thing (Keller), Smart Brevity (VandeHei), Make Time (Knapp), plus LinkedIn Learning courses on time management and agile estimation (see Notes). Evaluate two approaches, adopt one, and share with Michael by end of Q2. Time-block 3+ hrs/day for deep work from Q3. Publish 2 Jive posts sharing learnings with AIM org (Q2, Q3).

B. Delivery Discipline & Estimation Transparency
Maintain prioritized Jira backlog with estimates on all stories/epics. Improve estimation accuracy to within 20% on 75%+ of tasks by Q3. Cap concurrent workstreams at 3. Weekly Friday review: time audit, Jira status, priority alignment. No deadlines missed without re-scoping communicated 2+ weeks early. Review estimation trends in biweekly 1:1s. Share selective stop-doing list at mid-year and year-end.

C. Communication & Meeting Effectiveness
Before each meeting as presenter/key contributor, prepare 3-bullet outline: headline, ask/decision, one layer of detail on request. Lead with conclusions; let audience pull for depth. Pause 2-3 seconds before responding to reduce interrupting. Calibrate technical depth to audience. Complete Smart Brevity by Q2. Solicit feedback from 3-4 named peers (team + cross-team) at three checkpoints: Q2 baseline, mid-year, Q4. Document all feedback.

Success Criteria:
- Learning path complete per timeline (Notes)
- Time management system adopted by July 1
- Jira estimation within 20% on 75%+ tasks by Q3
- 90%+ adherence on pre-meeting prep and Friday reviews by Q3
- 2 Jive blog posts published
- 3 peer feedback rounds documented (Q2 baseline, mid-year, Q4)
- Manager confirmation of improvement at mid-year

- Measurable: Quantified targets (20% estimation accuracy, 75%+ task coverage, 90%+ adherence rates, 3+ hrs/day time-blocked, 2 blog posts, 3 peer feedback rounds).
- Achievable: Behavioral/process changes using existing tools (Jira, Outlook). Learning path totals ~8 hrs of courses plus 3 short books. Friday review adds ~15 min/week. Pre-meeting prep adds ~5-10 min/meeting.
- Relevant: Directly addresses all primary feedback from 2025 year-end review — overcommitment, poor estimation, boiling the ocean, over-detailing, interrupting, meeting preparedness, audience awareness.
- Time-bound: Milestones at April, May, June, Q3, Q4 (see timeline below).

LEARNING PATH

Books:
1. The One Thing — Gary Keller & Jay Papasan | Prioritization, time blocking, single-focus execution | By end of May
2. Smart Brevity — VandeHei, Allen & Schwartz | Concise professional communication, headline-first delivery | By end of Q2
3. Make Time — Jake Knapp & John Zeratsky (supplemental) | Daily Highlight method, flexible focus system | By end of Q3

LinkedIn Learning Courses:
1. Time Management Fundamentals — Dave Crenshaw (~1.5 hrs) | Time budgeting, task capture, cognitive clutter reduction | By end of May
2. Building a Time Management Mindset — Brad Aeon (~47 min) | Psychology of time control, calendar habits | By end of May
3. Agile at Work: Planning with Agile User Stories — Joshua Barnes (~51 min) | Story sizing, backlog prioritization, estimation techniques | By end of June

PEER FEEDBACK PLAN
Identify 3-4 named peers spanning different working relationships:
- 1-2 CIAM squad members (observe daily execution and Jira habits)
- 1 cross-team stakeholder (e.g., MSDE, ForgeRock, fixtures team — observe meeting behavior)
- 1 AIM peer outside CIAM (observe broader communication and collaboration)

Three structured rounds:
- Q2 Baseline (June): Establish starting point. Rate communication conciseness, meeting preparedness, pacing/interrupting, audience calibration. This becomes the benchmark.
- Mid-Year (Aug/Sep): Progress check. Same questions. Compare to baseline. Share results with Michael. Course-correct if needed.
- Q4 (Nov/Dec): Final assessment. Demonstrate improvement trajectory from baseline through year-end. Include in self-assessment.

Keep feedback lightweight — 3-4 questions, informal (Slack/email), takes peers <5 minutes. Document all responses.
