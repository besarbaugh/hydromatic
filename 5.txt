Build Disciplined Operating Habits in Execution, Time Management & Communication

Category: Culture & Values
CDRR Pillar 6: Talent & Engineering Excellence

Objective: Build disciplined habits in time management, delivery execution, and communication to directly address 2025 feedback on overcommitment, estimation accuracy, and message conciseness.

Focus Areas:

A. Time Management — Adopt and operationalize a single time-management system; protect daily deep-work time for priority workstreams.

B. Delivery Predictability — Improve delivery predictability through estimation accuracy, Jira hygiene, capped concurrency, and proactive re-scoping when commitments shift.

C. Communication Effectiveness — Improve communication effectiveness through headline-first messaging, structured meeting preparation, audience-calibrated depth, and peer feedback.

Success Criteria:
- Estimation within 20% on 75%+ of tasks by Q3
- Concurrency capped at 3 or fewer active workstreams
- 80%+ Say/Do completion rate on committed sprint deliverables by Q3
- 90%+ adherence to pre-meeting prep and weekly self-reviews
- Peer feedback on communication improvement collected and documented
- Manager confirms sustained improvement at mid-year


notes:

EXECUTION PLAN — Goal 5

This is the supporting execution plan shared with my manager. The goal text captures what gets evaluated; this captures how I execute it.

TIME MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
- Read Smart Brevity (VandeHei) by end of Q2 — doubles as communication framework
- Complete Time Management Fundamentals on LinkedIn Learning (Dave Crenshaw, ~1.5 hrs) by end of Q2
- Adopt daily time-blocking practice; protect 3+ hrs/day for deep work from Q3 onward

DELIVERY DISCIPLINE
- Maintain prioritized Jira backlog with effort estimates on all stories/epics
- Cap concurrent workstreams at 3 (WIP limit)
- Say/Do ratio: track % of committed sprint deliverables completed without carryover; target 80%+ by Q3
- Weekly Friday review (~15 min): time audit, Jira status, priority alignment
- No deadlines missed without re-scoping communicated 2+ weeks early
- Review completion trends in biweekly 1:1s with Michael
- Share selective stop-doing list at mid-year and year-end

COMMUNICATION
- Before each meeting as presenter/key contributor: 3-bullet prep (headline, ask/decision, one layer of detail on request)
- Lead with conclusions; let audience pull for depth
- Pause 2-3 seconds before responding to reduce interrupting
- Calibrate technical depth to audience

PEER FEEDBACK
- Identify 3 peers (mix of CIAM squad + cross-team)
- Mid-Year (Aug/Sep): lightweight 2-question Slack check-in on communication conciseness and meeting prep
- Year-End (Nov/Dec): same format, compare to mid-year, include in self-assessment

KNOWLEDGE SHARING
- Publish 1 Jive blog post (Q3) sharing adopted system and how it applies to Cloud IAM work

TIMELINE
Apr: Friday review and pre-meeting prep habits start. Begin Smart Brevity and LinkedIn Learning course.
May-Jun: Learning complete. Time-blocking system adopted. Say/Do baseline established.
Q3: System in daily practice. 80%+ Say/Do rate. Blog post published. Mid-year peer feedback. Manager confirmation.
Q4: Sustained adherence. Year-end peer feedback. Stop-doing list shared.
