 Transform Entra ID Application Lifecycle Management
Objective
Close AP_1197326 (due June 26, 2026) by eliminating persistent human ownership of Azure application registrations. Deliver custom RBAC roles and evolve SPTool into a self-service platform for temporary access grants, app configuration, and MFA opt-in—replacing standing Owner privileges entirely. Transition app registration creation to SystemID. Unify tagging across managed identities and enterprise app registrations to enable consistent lifecycle governance.
Workstreams
A.  Close AP_1197326 — Remove Human Owners from App Registrations
The AP requires self-service tooling to manage commonly used app registration fields without Owner permissions, followed by phased removal of Owners with an exception process. The approach centers on repurposing SPTool as the self-service platform for Entra ID application management:
•	Custom RBAC roles: Replace broad Owner entitlements with least-privilege grants scoped to what users actually need.
•	SPTool as self-service platform: Repurpose SPTool to provide temporary access grants validated against TAM (eonid + environment matching), expose app registration and enterprise app configuration options, and enable MFA opt-in for SaaS apps. Future capabilities include federated credential configuration and credential management. POC target: late April.
•	SystemID handoff: Move all new app registration creation to SystemID (ForgeRock front end).
•	Phased owner removal: Execute removal with documented exception process for approved exempt applications.
Longer term, SPTool is positioned to serve as an MCP server, enabling integration with the AI-driven workload intelligence model (Goal 4) to surface recommended configuration changes and developer enablement guidance directly through the tool.
B.  Tagging Unification — Managed Identities & App Registrations
Align the tagging taxonomy across managed identities and enterprise app/app registrations to enable consistent reporting, ownership tracking, and orphan detection. This is a prerequisite for scalable lifecycle governance and feeds directly into the AI-driven workload intelligence initiative planned under a separate goal.
•	Define a unified tagging standard that reconciles managed identity metadata with app registration metadata.
•	Implement tagging enforcement automation and track compliance.
•	Surface orphaned or untagged identities for remediation.

•	AP_1197326 closure evidence submitted by June 26 deadline.

•	100% of new app registrations created via SystemID.
•	Phased Owner removal underway; exception process documented and operational.
•	Unified tagging standard published; enforcement automation deployed; compliance tracked.

Business Value
Eliminates a systemic risk vector by removing persistent human ownership of cloud app registrations across the Firm’s Entra ID tenants. SPTool’s evolution into a self-service platform—covering temporary access, configuration management, and MFA opt-in—reduces standing privilege while giving application teams a better experience than portal access. Unified tagging establishes the data foundation for consistent lifecycle governance and enables the AI-driven workload intelligence initiative to operate on clean, standardized metadata. The future MCP server integration creates a path for AI-recommended configuration changes to flow directly to developers through the tooling they already use.
