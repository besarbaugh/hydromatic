Establish a unified identity metadata model spanning Azure and GCP, and extend CIDB to provide a single authoritative view of cloud identity relationships across the Firm's tenants.

Identity metadata is fragmented: ForgeRock-created app registrations use custom security attributes, managed identities use Azure resource tags, and GCP service accounts follow separate conventions. No unified view ties these together or relates them to federation and consent configurations. CIDB ingests from multiple sources but lacks identity relationship data.

Key Deliverables
- Define unified identity model spanning Entra app registrations, enterprise apps, managed identities, and GCP service accounts.
- Collaborate with GCP team to align tagging standards and metadata capture across platforms.
- Extend CIDB with identity relationship data covering federation, consent, and ownership for Azure and GCP.
- Evaluate Terraform-backed service identity provisioning for IaC-managed, auditable identity lifecycle.
- Surface orphaned or inconsistently tagged identities across both clouds for remediation.
- Establish clean data foundation for future AI-driven workload intelligence and MCP-based tooling.

Success Criteria
- Unified identity metadata model defined (Azure + GCP) by September 2026.
- CIDB extended with multi-cloud identity relationship data by November 2026.
- GCP tagging alignment documented and enforcement underway by Q4 2026.
- Orphaned/inconsistent identities surfaced across both clouds by December 2026.

Timeline
Q3: Unified model defined. GCP tagging collaboration. CIDB integration underway. Terraform evaluation.
Q4: CIDB data available across Azure and GCP. Orphaned identities surfaced for remediation.

Business Value
Establishes multi-cloud lifecycle governance and a unified identity data foundation enabling consistent oversight, orphan detection, and future AI-driven workload intelligence across the Firm's cloud footprint.
