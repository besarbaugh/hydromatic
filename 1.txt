Establish a unified identity metadata model and extend CIDB to provide a single authoritative view of Entra identity relationships across the Firm's tenants.

Today, identity metadata is fragmented: ForgeRock-created app registrations and enterprise apps use custom security attributes, while managed identities rely on Azure resource tags. There is no unified view that ties these objects together as Entra identities or relates them to federation and consent configurations. CIDB (CloudDataInventory on Snowflake) already ingests data from Azure Graph, AWS, TAI DW, and custom metadata sources—but lacks identity relationship data.

Key Deliverables
- Define a unified identity model that relates app registrations, enterprise apps, and managed identities regardless of metadata source (custom security attributes vs. resource tags).
- Extend CIDB to include identity relationship data, enabling a single view of Entra identity objects tied to their federation, consent, and ownership metadata.
- Surface orphaned, untagged, or inconsistently tagged identities for remediation and governance reporting.
- Establish a clean data foundation to support future AI-driven workload intelligence and MCP-based tooling integration.

Success Criteria
- Unified identity metadata model defined by September 2026.
- CIDB extended with identity relationship data by November 2026.
- Orphaned and inconsistently tagged identities surfaced and remediation underway by December 2026.

Target Timeline
Q3: Unified identity metadata model defined. CIDB integration underway.
Q4: CIDB identity data available. Orphaned and inconsistent identities surfaced for remediation.

Business Value
Establishes durable lifecycle governance controls and a unified identity data foundation, enabling consistent oversight, orphan detection, and future AI-driven workload intelligence and configuration recommendations.
