PERFORMANCE & IMPACT
GOAL 1
Transform Entra ID Application Lifecycle Management
Objective
Eliminate persistent human ownership of Entra ID application registrations across the Firm’s tenants by closing AP_1197326 by June 26, 2026. Replace standing Owner privileges with least-privilege, time-bound access through custom RBAC roles and self-service tooling, transition app registration creation to SystemID, and establish a unified identity metadata model to enable scalable lifecycle governance.
Key Deliverables
A.  Close AP_1197326 — Remove Human Owners from App Registrations
Deliver a self-service operating model that enables application teams to manage required app registration and enterprise app settings without Owner permissions, followed by phased Owner removal with a documented exception process.
–	Custom RBAC roles: Define and deploy least-privilege roles scoped to required app management functions.
–	SPTool self-service platform: Enable TAM-validated temporary access grants (eonid + environment matching), app registration and enterprise app configuration, and MFA opt-in for SaaS apps. POC by late April; production by June.
–	SystemID handoff: Transition all new app registration creation to SystemID (ForgeRock front end).
–	Phased Owner removal: Execute removal across eligible apps with an approved exception process for exempt use cases.
B.  Unified Identity Metadata & CIDB Integration
Address fragmented identity metadata by establishing a unified model across app registrations, enterprise apps, and managed identities, and extending CIDB to provide a single authoritative view of Entra identity relationships.
–	Define a unified identity model across metadata sources (custom security attributes and Azure resource tags).
–	Extend CIDB to include identity relationship data spanning federation, consent, and ownership.
–	Surface orphaned, untagged, or inconsistently tagged identities for remediation.
–	Establish a clean data foundation to support future AI-driven workload intelligence and MCP-based tooling integration.
Success Criteria
•	AP_1197326 closure evidence submitted by June 26, 2026.
•	SPTool in production as self-service platform (temporary access, app configuration, MFA opt-in) by June 2026.
•	Custom RBAC roles deployed by May 2026; users manage app settings without Owner permissions.
•	100% of new app registrations created through SystemID by end of June 2026.
•	Phased Owner removal underway by July 2026; >80% of eligible apps remediated by December 2026.
•	Unified identity metadata model defined by September 2026; CIDB extended with identity relationship data by November 2026.
Target Timeline

•	Custom RBAC role definitions finalized
•	SystemID workflow in testing

•	SPTool promoted to production

•	Continue phased Owner removal; track progress

•	Owner removal >80% of eligible apps
Business Value
Removes a systemic identity risk by eliminating persistent human ownership of cloud app registrations while preserving developer velocity through self-service, time-bound access. Establishes durable lifecycle governance controls and a unified identity data foundation, enabling consistent oversight, orphan detection, and future AI-driven workload intelligence and configuration recommendations.
