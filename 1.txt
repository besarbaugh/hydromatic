PERFORMANCE & IMPACT
GOAL 1
Transform Entra ID Application Lifecycle Management
Objective
Eliminate persistent human ownership of Entra ID application registrations across the Firm’s tenants by closing AP_1197326 by June 26, 2026. Replace standing Owner privileges with least-privilege, time-bound access through custom RBAC roles and self-service tooling. Transition app registration creation to SystemID and establish a unified identity metadata model to enable scalable lifecycle governance.
Key Deliverables
A.  Close AP_1197326 — Remove Human Owners from App Registrations
Deliver a self-service model that enables application teams to manage required app registration and enterprise app settings without Owner permissions, followed by phased Owner removal with a documented exception process.
–	Custom RBAC roles: Define and deploy least-privilege roles scoped to required app management functions.
–	SPTool self-service platform: Repurpose SPTool for temporary access grants validated against TAM (eonid + environment matching), app registration and enterprise app configuration, and MFA opt-in for SaaS apps. POC by late April; production by June.
–	SystemID handoff: Transition all new app registration creation to SystemID (ForgeRock front end).
–	Phased Owner removal: Execute removal across eligible apps with an approved exception process for exempt use cases.
B.  Unified Identity Metadata & CIDB Integration
Today, identity metadata is fragmented: ForgeRock-created app registrations and enterprise apps use custom security attributes, while managed identities rely on Azure resource tags. There is no unified view that ties these objects together as Entra identities or relates them to federation and consent configurations. CIDB (CloudDataInventory on Snowflake) already ingests data from Azure Graph, AWS, TAI DW, and custom metadata sources—but lacks identity relationship data.
–	Define a unified identification model that relates app registrations, enterprise apps, and managed identities regardless of metadata source (custom security attributes vs. resource tags).
–	Extend CIDB to include identity relationship data, enabling a single view of Entra identity objects tied to their federation, consent, and ownership metadata.
–	Surface orphaned, untagged, or inconsistently tagged identities for remediation.
–	Establish this as the clean data foundation for the AI-driven workload intelligence initiative, where unified metadata and the least-privilege access model built in Workstream A provide the groundwork for eventual MCP-based tooling integration.
Success Criteria
•	AP_1197326 closure evidence submitted by June 26, 2026.
•	SPTool in production as self-service platform (temporary access, app configuration, MFA opt-in) by June 2026.
•	Custom RBAC roles deployed by May 2026; users manage app settings without Owner permissions.
•	100% of new app registrations created through SystemID by end of June 2026.
•	Phased Owner removal underway by July 2026; >80% of eligible apps remediated by December 2026.
•	Unified identity metadata model defined by September 2026; CIDB extended with identity relationship data by November 2026.

Business Value
Removes a systemic identity risk by eliminating persistent human ownership of cloud app registrations while preserving developer velocity through self-service, time-bound access. Establishes durable lifecycle governance controls and a clean metadata foundation to support AI-driven workload intelligence and configuration recommendations.
